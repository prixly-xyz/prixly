Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 June 2025  ·  Effective immediately  ·  Contact: privacy.prixly.xyz@gmail.com

Prixly is a price comparison service. We show you prices from third-party retailers and redirect you to their websites to complete any purchase. We are not a retailer and we do not process, store, or have any visibility into your payment information or order details.

1. Who We Are

Prixly ("we", "us", "our") operates the price comparison service available at prixly.xyz. For any privacy-related questions, contact us at privacy.prixly.xyz@gmail.com.

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Data You Provide Directly

  • Search queries — the text you type into the search bar (e.g. "iPhone 15 Pro", "Nike Air Max 90"). This is used solely to retrieve and display price results.
  • Product URLs — if you paste a product link into the search bar, that URL is processed to identify the product. It is transmitted to Anthropic's API where slug parsing alone is insufficient.
  • Images and screenshots — if you upload or drop an image to use our visual search feature, that image is sent to Anthropic's Claude Vision API to extract a product description. Images are not stored by Prixly at any point.
  • Account registration data — if you create a Prixly account, we collect your name, email address, and a password you choose. Your password is stored in hashed form and never in plain text. This data is used solely to authenticate you and personalise your experience.
  • Wishlist items — products you save to your wishlist are stored against your account. This data is used to display your wishlist and to trigger price-drop notifications when a watched item falls below your target price.
  • Email address (optional) — if you sign up for price alerts, we collect the email address you provide in order to send you alert notifications. This is strictly opt-in. We will not use your email for marketing unless you separately consent to that.
  • Target price for alerts (optional) — the price threshold you set when creating an alert. Stored only to trigger notifications at the right time.

2.2 Data Collected Automatically

  • Browser timezone and locale — read from your browser at page load to auto-detect your region and suggest an appropriate currency. This is not transmitted to our servers; it is processed client-side in your browser only.
  • Language preference — detected from your browser's language settings and saved to your browser's localStorage so the site remembers your preference on your next visit.
  • Region and currency preference — if you manually select a region or currency, that preference is saved to your browser's localStorage.
  • Recent searches — the last six search terms you enter are saved to your browser's localStorage to populate the "recent searches" shortcut row. This data never leaves your device.

2.3 What We Do Not Collect

  • Payment card details, bank information, or any financial data — all purchases happen on the retailer's website, not ours.
  • Purchase history or order confirmations — once you click through to a retailer, we have no visibility into what you do there.
  • Tracking cookies or cross-site advertising identifiers — we do not use any advertising or behavioural tracking cookies.
  • Precise geolocation — we never request GPS or precise location access.

  • Email and alert preferences — retained for as long as your price alert is active. Deleted promptly upon unsubscribe or alert cancellation.

  • Legal bases: Our processing of Brazilian users' data relies on legitimate interest (Article 7(IX) LGPD) for search queries and service operation, and consent (Article 7(I) LGPD) for email alert sign-up and image uploads. Consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous.
  • Your rights under LGPD (Article 18): confirmation of the existence of processing; access to your data; correction of incomplete or inaccurate data; anonymisation, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary data; data portability; deletion of data processed with consent; information about public and private entities with whom we share data; information about the possibility of withholding consent and its consequences; and revocation of consent.
  • Cross-border transfers: Data sent to Anthropic's API (based in the USA) constitutes an international transfer under LGPD Article 33. This transfer is carried out under contractual clauses that provide a level of protection equivalent to that required by Brazilian law, consistent with Anthropic's Data Processing Agreement.
  • National Data Protection Authority (ANPD): Brazilian users may lodge a complaint with the Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados at gov.br/anpd.
  • Data Protection Officer: Prixly does not currently have a formal DPO registered with the ANPD. As the service scales to Brazilian users, we will appoint one and update this page. In the interim, all data requests should be directed to privacy.prixly.xyz@gmail.com.

12.2 Canada — PIPEDA and Quebec Law 25

Canada's federal Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) applies to Prixly's collection and use of personal data from Canadian users in the course of commercial activity. Quebec users are additionally protected by Law 25 (Act to Modernize Privacy Sector Enterprises, in force September 2023), which is the strictest provincial privacy law in Canada.

  • Accountability: Prixly is accountable for all personal data in its possession, including data transferred to Anthropic for processing. We have designated privacy.prixly.xyz@gmail.com as the point of contact for all Canadian privacy matters.
  • Identifying purpose: The purpose for which personal data is collected is identified at or before the time of collection (see Section 2 of this page).
  • Consent: We obtain meaningful consent for data collection. For data collected automatically (e.g. browser locale), consent is implied by use of the service; for email sign-up and image uploads, consent is express.
  • Cross-border transfers (Quebec Law 25 — Article 17): Before transferring personal data outside Quebec, we are required to conduct a Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA). A PIA has been carried out for the transfer of search queries and images to Anthropic (USA). The assessment concluded that the transfer is justified given Anthropic's contractual safeguards. The PIA is available on request to Quebec residents.
  • Your rights: Canadian users have the right to access their personal data, to challenge the accuracy of that data, and to withdraw consent at any time (subject to legal or contractual restrictions). Under Quebec Law 25, you also have the right to data portability and the right to request that automated decisions affecting you be explained.
  • Privacy Commissioner of Canada: Federal complaints may be directed to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada. Quebec complaints may be directed to the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec (CAI).
  • Breach notification: In the event of a privacy breach that poses a real risk of significant harm to Canadian users, we will notify both affected individuals and the Privacy Commissioner of Canada as required under PIPEDA's breach reporting obligations.

12.3 Australia — Privacy Act 1988 (Australian Privacy Principles)

The Australian Privacy Act 1988 and its thirteen Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) apply to Prixly's handling of personal information about Australian users. The Privacy Act applies to overseas organisations that carry on a business in Australia or collect personal information from individuals in Australia.

  • Open and transparent management (APP 1): This page, together with our Privacy Policy, constitutes Prixly's APP Privacy Policy, setting out how we manage personal information.
  • Anonymity (APP 2): Where lawful and practicable, Australian users may interact with Prixly anonymously. Searching for prices does not require you to identify yourself. Only price alert sign-up requires an email address.
  • Collection notice (APP 5): At the time of collecting personal information, we notify you of: our identity and contact details; the purposes of collection; any third parties to whom we disclose information; and your rights of access and correction. This page constitutes that notice.
  • Cross-border disclosures (APP 8): Before disclosing personal information to an overseas recipient (Anthropic, USA), we have taken reasonable steps to ensure the recipient does not breach the APPs in relation to that information. Anthropic's DPA and Standard Contractual Clauses provide these assurances. Australian users should be aware that overseas recipients may not be subject to Australian privacy law, and Prixly may not be accountable under the Privacy Act for any breach by that overseas recipient — however, we contractually require Anthropic to handle data to a standard consistent with Australian law.
  • Your rights: Australian users have the right to access personal information we hold about them (APP 12) and to request correction of inaccurate, out-of-date, or incomplete information (APP 13). Requests should be sent to privacy.prixly.xyz@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.
  • Complaints: If you are not satisfied with our response to a privacy complaint, you may refer the matter to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).
  • Sensitive information: Prixly does not intentionally collect sensitive information as defined under the Privacy Act (including health, racial, political, or religious information). If you include such information in a search query, it is processed transiently and not retained.

12.4 India — Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP Act)

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP Act) regulates the processing of digital personal data of individuals in India. It applies to Prixly as a Data Fiduciary processing personal data of Indian users, whether that processing occurs in India or outside India.

  • Data Fiduciary: Under the DPDP Act, Prixly is the Data Fiduciary — the entity that determines the purpose and means of processing your personal data.
  • Consent (Section 6, DPDP Act): Where we rely on consent as the legal basis for processing (email sign-up, image uploads), we obtain free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous consent through a clear affirmative action. You may withdraw consent at any time, and withdrawal will not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • Notice (Section 5, DPDP Act): At or before the point of collecting personal data with consent, we provide notice of: the personal data to be collected; the purpose of processing; and the manner in which consent may be withdrawn. This page and our Privacy Policy constitute that notice.
  • Legitimate use: For processing that does not rely on consent (e.g. processing search queries to return results), we rely on legitimate use as defined under Section 7 of the DPDP Act — specifically, the performance of a function that is reasonably expected by the Data Principal (the user).
  • Your rights as a Data Principal (Chapter III, DPDP Act):
    • Right to access information about personal data processed (Section 11).
    • Right to correction and erasure of personal data (Section 12).
    • Right to grievance redressal (Section 13) — contact privacy.prixly.xyz@gmail.com for any grievance; we will respond within 30 days.
    • Right to nominate another person to exercise your rights in the event of death or incapacity (Section 14).
  • Cross-border transfers: The DPDP Act permits transfer of personal data outside India except to countries specifically restricted by the Indian Government. As of the date of this policy, the Government of India has not issued a negative list of restricted countries. Data transferred to Anthropic (USA) is therefore currently permitted. We will monitor any notifications issued under Section 16 of the DPDP Act and update our practices accordingly.
  • Data localisation: The DPDP Act does not currently impose blanket data localisation requirements. We will comply with any sector-specific localisation rules that may apply as the law is further notified.
  • Breach notification: In the event of a personal data breach affecting Indian users, we will notify the Data Protection Board of India and affected Data Principals in accordance with Section 8(6) of the DPDP Act.
  • Data Protection Board of India: If your grievance is not resolved to your satisfaction, you may approach the Data Protection Board of India once it is formally constituted and operational under the DPDP Act.
